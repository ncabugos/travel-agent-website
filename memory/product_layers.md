EAH revenue compounds across three product layers, each launching in a different phase:

**Layer 1 — SaaS (live now, public Phase 2 ~Sep–Oct 2026).** Tier matrix is LOCKED: Starter $89/$499, Growth $179/$1,499, Custom $349/$2,999, Agency from $899/$4,999. Locked in `CLAUDE.md` §3 and authoritative in `Elite_Advisor_Hub_Feature_Matrix.xlsx`.

**Layer 2 — Studio (Phase 3, ~Nov 2026–Feb 2027, Month 9+).** Content syndication: operator-produced articles, social posts, email templates that advisors rebrand and push to their own clients. Plans: Lite $79, Pro $149, Agency $399+. Sold standalone OR bundled with SaaS. **Why:** Why: infinite operating leverage — one Claude-produced article rides 40 advisor distributions. How to apply: do NOT launch Studio at SaaS launch; wait until 25+ advisors are on platform so there is a distribution network to syndicate through.

**Layer 3 — Supplier Partnerships (Year 2, ~May 2027+).** Sponsored content network: luxury suppliers pay EAH for placements distributed to advisor network with FTC §255 disclosure. Packages: $2,500/mo Featured, $5,000 Sponsored Article, $15,000 Campaign, $50,000+ Annual. **Why:** the media-network business layered on the SaaS — the same model luxury travel publications run, but with advisor-level distribution instead of consumer-level. **How to apply:** requires 30+ advisor installed base first; until then, run informal featured placements with key suppliers to build relationships that become paid Year-2 contracts.

**Layer 4 (separate) — Aurora / Signature tier (Months 6–10 dogfood, Month 9+ paid).** $599/mo + $7,499 setup. Capped at 5 paid Signature advisors in year one. Year 2 converts to $200/mo add-on for existing Custom advisors. **Why:** Aurora has different unit economics (Claude API per advisor) and a different sales motion (application/interview, not self-serve) — must be a separate Stripe SKU from day one, not a Custom checkbox. **How to apply:** dogfood on wineandwellnesstravel.com first; Eden (edenforyourworld.com) is likely first paid Signature.

**Editorial firewall (applies to Layers 2–3).** Operator (Nick) approves every sponsored or syndicated asset. Suppliers do not get direct platform access. FTC disclosure copy is operator-controlled, not advisor-editable. The same quality bar applies as organic journal posts — this is the only thing that protects the brand at the top-1% positioning the marketing site is being rewritten to.
